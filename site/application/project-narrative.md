# Project Narrative

> **Status:** Not started · **Owner:** Unassigned · **Deadline:** 2026-04-28

## Requirements

- **Page limit:** 5 pages technical (includes charts, graphs, maps, photos). Does **NOT** count: Title Page, Budget/Justification, biosketches, publications/references, appendices, optional TOC.
- **Format:** 8.5 × 11, 1-inch margins, body text ≥ 11 pt.
- **No URLs** supplying supplementary content — reviewers are not required to access external links.
- **No AI-generated narratives.** Disclose AI tool use (unless solely for editing original draft).

## Must address

- Vision for the science/applied-energy initiatives to be pursued.
- How and why AI will enhance the scientific and technical workflows.
- How the work advances or solves the chosen **focus area** and the corresponding **challenge**.
- Phase I focus: demonstrate **which elements are needed to achieve an AI advantage**.

## Phase I application requirements (all applications)

- Specify the realistic scientific/engineering workflow aspects accelerated or improved by the proposed AI, and explain why new AI capabilities are more promising than alternatives.
- Describe the full pipeline: data generation & curation; experiments and observation; AI model training, fine-tuning, inference; integration (agentic or otherwise) into a capability useful for science or engineering.
- If data is sensitive or proprietary, describe how data preparation and/or AI training will protect sensitive aspects while maximizing the value of the resulting AI capability.

## Buy America statement

Within the first **2 pages**, include a short statement on whether the project involves construction, alteration, maintenance, or repair of public infrastructure in the United States. See [IX.C.2 in the RFA](../rfa/pages/9-other-information.md) for definitions.

Importance and relevance. Which aspect of the chosen challenge will be addressed. Set proposed research in perspective to other efforts in the field. Novel or unique aspects. Relevant literature cited.

### Project Objectives (~0.5 page)

Clear, concise statement of specific objectives. Alignment with chosen focus topic. How objectives lead to an **AI advantage**.

### Proposed Research and Methods (~1.5 pages)

Research plan for a **9-month project**. Activities and methods. Enough technical detail to evaluate impact. For each activity: responsible key investigator(s) and associated budget. If building on an existing DOE project, describe how this submission leverages and is distinct from it.

*[content]*

### Milestones in the Nine Months (~1 page)

List of clearly defined, measurable milestones.

*[content]*

### Data Sources and Models (~0.5 page)

Existing or new data sources. Team's access to the data. AI models or frameworks to be used. **Do not** describe computing resources here — that goes in [Appendix 2](appendix-2-facilities.md).

*[content]*

### Decision Gate Metrics (~0.5 page)

Specific metrics for Phase I go/no-go decision. **AI advantage metrics encouraged** — e.g., scaling behavior showing performance improves with more data/compute/resources. Quantitative statements preferred.

*[content]*

## Collaborative applications

If we end up as part of a multi-institutional team: **every institution submits an identical common narrative**, including all appendices. Budget and budget justification are unique per institution but may reference parts of the common narrative. Every Project Narrative in the team must be identical (including the title).
